# ADR 015 — Family-Shareable, Not Child-Directed: COPPA by Positioning and Architecture

- **ID:** YYBW-015
- **Status:** Decided
- **Date:** 2026-04-13
- **Scope:** YY Branching World / YY's World evolution
- **Depends on:** YYBW-002, YYBW-007, YYBW-009
- **Supersedes / clarifies:** Clarifies relationship to Case 002 and COPPA concerns

## Context

There was concern that the product might become too cutesy and accidentally look child-directed. At the same time, the intention is that peers might selectively share it with their families. Existing YY properties and Case 002 both suggest a better framing: adult-facing, reflective, family-shareable, outward-only, and low-collection.

## Decision

The product is positioned primarily for adults/peers, with selective family sharing. It should remain warm and simple without presenting itself as a kids product. Architecture should remain outward-facing and low-data, especially at first.

## Why

This:
- respects COPPA realities
- fits the actual voice of yyand.me and Case 002
- preserves creative freedom
- avoids unnecessary liability and compliance burden

## Alternatives considered

1. **Explicit kids-first product** — rejected as high-risk and not actually necessary.
2. **Sterile adult technical tool** — rejected because warmth and accessibility matter.
3. **Hidden ambiguity about audience** — rejected because positioning needs deliberate clarity.

## Reversals / scars preserved

- The cute/childlike temptation is preserved as a design risk.
- The Calvin-and-Hobbes model remains useful: speaks simply, can be read at multiple levels.
- Case 002 remains the philosophical ancestor, but this site should not simply mimic a 'for kids' surface.

## Consequences

- Copy, visuals, and thumbnails should lean thoughtful over toy-like.
- Avoid child-specific data collection, mechanics, or gamified onboarding.
- Adults should remain the explicit primary user in copy and GTM.

## Invariants preserved

Discipline via audience clarity; Survivability and Explainability through structurally simple, outward-facing architecture.

## Freshness boundary

Revisit if account systems or direct child-facing features are ever introduced.
