# ADR 016 — Distribution: YouTube Shorts, Daily Email, and Site as the Home of Depth

- **ID:** YYBW-016
- **Status:** Decided
- **Date:** 2026-04-13
- **Scope:** YY Branching World / YY's World evolution
- **Depends on:** YYBW-003, YYBW-007, YYBW-008, YYBW-009
- **Supersedes / clarifies:** Clarifies go-to-market and notification strategy

## Context

The product is easier to show than explain. YouTube Shorts emerged as the best top-of-funnel: same event, multiple branches, quick outcome. Email emerged later as a Substack-like daily note for people following a branch. The site remains the place for depth and inspection.

## Decision

Use a three-layer distribution model:
- **YouTube Shorts** for discovery
- **Site** for timeline exploration and diff inspection
- **Opt-in email** for daily/interesting-day updates on followed branches

## Why

This matches the product's strengths:
- episodic
- visual/comparative
- habit-forming
- easy to serialize

## Alternatives considered

1. **App/site only** — rejected as too hard for initial concept transmission.
2. **Substack as the core product** — rejected because Substack is not the right programmable runtime.
3. **Long-form video first** — rejected because Shorts are the cleanest demonstration unit.

## Reversals / scars preserved

- The YouTube insight materially changed go-to-market.
- The product is not just software; it is also a content engine.
- Email is a push layer for attachment, not the first explanation layer.

## Consequences

- Need export-ready artifacts for Shorts and emails.
- Need careful cadence; not every day merits rich outbound messaging.
- The site must remain the source of truth and depth even if distribution channels grow.

## Invariants preserved

Compression by one-event one-comparison Shorts; Explainability through side-by-side comparison; Discipline through channel roles.

## Freshness boundary

Stable for launch path.
