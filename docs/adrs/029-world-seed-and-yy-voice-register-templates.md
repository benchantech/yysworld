# ADR 029 — World Seed and YY Voice Register: Designed Now, Introduced at Next Root

- **ID:** YYBW-029
- **Status:** Decided
- **Date:** 2026-04-15
- **Scope:** YY Branching World
- **Depends on:** YYBW-003, YYBW-005, YYBW-011
- **Supersedes / clarifies:** Extends YYBW-011 (versioned canon) and YYBW-005 (event stream) with new authoring artifacts

---

## Context

The April 2026 root was initialized without two things that would significantly improve both narrative quality and pipeline consistency:

**1. A world seed** — named, stable geography and seasonal physics that persist across all days of a root. Without this, each daily generation invents locations and conditions from scratch. Locations that appear in one day vanish the next; the world feels arbitrary rather than accumulated.

**2. A voice register** — specific, documented patterns for how YY actually sounds. The character baseline (`yy_baseline.json`) captures psychological profile as trait scores, but not the prose signature that makes YY recognizable. The first full root generated evidence for what that signature is; that evidence should be captured before it fades.

The question was whether to retrofit these onto the April root or design them properly for next month.

---

## Decision

### 1. Design both templates now; introduce both at the next root transition

The April 2026 root does not get a world seed or voice register. It runs to completion with the existing baseline.

At the start of the next monthly root (May 2026 or later), both are introduced:
- The world seed is authored once before `pipeline:init` is run for that root, committed alongside the first branch files
- The v1.1 voice register is added to `yy_baseline.json` (as new sections, preserving existing fields) and declared as the active version

This respects ADR-003 (roots are finite arcs) and ADR-011 (YY evolves explicitly at version boundaries). The April root is not retroactively changed.

### 2. World seed: per-root, authored by the human author before initialization

The world seed (`world-seed.json`) is a new optional file in `runs/{rootId}/`. It is:
- Authored once, before the pipeline runs Day 1
- Not generated by the pipeline — it is authorial input, like the inbox
- Read by the pipeline during generation to provide stable geography, seasonal context, and social conditions
- Not mutated during the run (it is the root's starting conditions, not its current state)

Template: `docs/templates/world-seed.json`

When the pipeline reads a world seed, it uses:
- `geography.named_places` — to keep location names consistent across days
- `seasonal_physics` — to calibrate what "adequate" food means this month
- `social_field.known_presences` — to make recurring animals recognizable
- `atmosphere` — to find appropriate event translations

When no world seed is present, the pipeline generates freely as it does now.

### 3. Voice register: per-YY-version, evidence-based, introduced at v1.1

The voice register (`docs/yy/voice-v1.1-draft.md`) documents the prose patterns that emerged from the first actual artifacts. It is separate from the trait baseline because traits are psychological properties; voice patterns are expressive habits. They are related but not the same thing.

At v1.1, the voice register becomes an input to the pipeline system prompt alongside the trait baseline. The pipeline already reads the baseline to construct the system prompt (`generate.ts` → `systemPrompt()`); v1.1 extends this to include the voice register.

The voice register documents:
- The three-beat sentence rhythm
- Sensory priority order (light → sound → physical → abstract)
- Recurring internal figures (`the math`, `the general situation`, etc.)
- State-dependent register (depleted / adequate / engaged)
- Voice failure boundaries (parallel to character failure boundaries)
- Reference quotes from actual artifacts

It is marked "draft" until the transition. New patterns discovered during the April run should be added to the draft before it is promoted to active.

### 4. The transition

At next root start, in order:

1. Author the world seed from the template and commit it
2. Update `yy_baseline.json` with voice register sections (marking as `yy_version: 1.1`)
3. Declare the v1.1 baseline in the root's branch files
4. Update the pipeline system prompt to include the voice register
5. ADR-011 applies: the April root continues to reference v1.0; the new root references v1.1

---

## Why design now rather than at the transition

The voice patterns are observable now, while the artifacts are fresh. Writing the voice register after 30 days of generation without it risks documenting drift rather than the original calibration. Evidence captured while it's recent is more accurate than evidence recalled later.

The world seed template is structural — it takes an afternoon to design correctly. Designing it under time pressure at the start of a new month invites cutting corners. Having the template ready means the authoring session is filling in known fields, not designing a schema.

---

## Why not retrofit April

The April root's artifacts were generated without a world seed. The geography that emerged (east cache, fence post, main path) did so organically, not from a documented source of truth. Retrofitting a world seed to match what happened would produce a document that describes outputs rather than inputs — a reconstruction, not a source.

More importantly: the geography is actually in a state that needs a month of observation to document properly. What's the east cache's relationship to home? Is the fence post on the main path or elsewhere? The world seed for a root should be written before generation, not after. April runs without one; May starts with one.

---

## What this does not do

- Does not retroactively change any April artifacts
- Does not require world seeds (they are optional; roots without them generate freely)
- Does not freeze the voice register — it is a living document, updated as more artifacts are written
- Does not change the character calibration process (22-scenario interview for new characters remains the standard)

---

## Consequences

- `docs/templates/world-seed.json` is the canonical template for all future roots
- `docs/yy/voice-v1.1-draft.md` is the in-progress voice register; promoted to active at v1.1 introduction
- The pipeline (`generate.ts`, `pipeline-go.md`) needs to be updated at the transition to read both new inputs
- The ADR-028 antipattern registry in `craft.md` is the forward-compatible precursor to the voice register — it captures failure modes; the register captures what success looks like

## Freshness boundary

Revisit if a second character is introduced — their voice register and world seed may overlap with YY's or diverge from it. The template is designed for YY specifically; a second character might need a different schema.
