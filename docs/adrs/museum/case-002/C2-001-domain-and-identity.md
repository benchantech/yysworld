# C2-001: Domain & Identity — YY's World
**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp
**Status:** Decided
**Date:** March 30, 2026
**Position in Hierarchy:** Foundational — all downstream ADRs assume YY as protagonist and YY's World as the domain
**Freshness Boundary:** Permanent. Expires only if YY ceases to be the protagonist or domain is changed.

---

## Capture

The site is named YY's World. "YY's World." The possessive is intentional — this is not a site about YY, it is YY's space. YY the stuffed squirrel, established character across yyand.me and yymethod.com, extends into a world built for kids.

The domain continues an existing universe rather than creating a new one. YY already has provenance: 7 podcast episodes, a documented creative partnership, a known preference for acorns, and a paper trail of disagreements with the operator.

---

## Why

A new character with no history would require the site to establish credibility from zero. YY arrives with provenance. Kids encounter not a mascot invented for their consumption but a character with an existing record — one who has already disagreed with the operator, hidden the metronome, and been overruled (and occasionally been right).

The possessive "YY's World" does more work than "YY Method for Kids" or "Learn with YY." It does not describe a method. It does not announce a lesson. It claims a space. Kids who visit are entering somewhere — not being enrolled in something.

The domain continuity with yymethod.com and yyand.me also signals to adults — parents, educators, curious readers — that this exists within a coherent universe with an operator who has a documented identity and methodology. Provenance extends in both directions.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not yymethod-kids.com?**
Describes the method, not the character. Announces "this is educational content about a methodology." Signals lesson before the kid has read a single word. Defeats the stealth inoculation (see C2-008).

**Why not yylearns.com or similar?**
"Learns" is the worst word. Kids do not want to go somewhere to learn. They want to go somewhere interesting. Learning is what happens; it should not be the name.

**Why not yyworld.com?**
Likely taken or too generic. The possessive "YY's World" is also more specific — it belongs to YY personally, not to some abstract world concept.

**Why not a completely separate brand with no YY connection?**
Destroys the provenance advantage. A standalone brand requires establishing credibility, character, and history from scratch. YY already has all three. The connection to the adult YY universe also gives older readers — a kid who ages into the method — a natural continuity path.

## Commit

**Decision:** YY's World. YY's World. The possessive claims the space. YY the stuffed squirrel is the protagonist with existing provenance. No new character to establish.

**Confidence:** High.
