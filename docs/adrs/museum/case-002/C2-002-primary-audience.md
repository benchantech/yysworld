# C2-002: Primary Audience — Kids, Not Parents
**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp
**Status:** Decided
**Date:** March 30, 2026
**Position in Hierarchy:** Foundational — content register, tone, and inoculation mechanism all depend on this decision
**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Expires if the site pivots to a family or educator audience.

---

## Capture

The primary audience is kids, approximately ages 8–14. The site speaks to them directly. It does not explain itself to their parents. It does not position itself as a curriculum for their teachers. It is not a family site. It is not an educational platform. It is a place kids discover and return to on their own.

---

## Why

The cognitive inoculation this site delivers works at formation stage. A child developing reasoning habits is a different target than an adult whose patterns are already calcified. The antibody to cognitive theft — the reflex to ask "why this and not that, and what breaks this claim?" — is most efficiently planted before the habits of passive consumption are fully formed.

Adults who need this protection can encounter the YY Method through yymethod.com or home.yymethod.com. YY's World is not for them. The sites are designed for different moments in the formation arc.

The direct address to kids also changes what the content can be. A site that speaks to parents about kids is hedged, explained, permission-seeking. A site that speaks directly to kids can be zany, specific, and structurally weird in ways that adults might find unpolished. That structural weirdness is load-bearing — it is what carries the inoculation without announcing it.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not a family audience?**
A family audience requires content that makes sense to both kids and parents simultaneously. That register is necessarily hedged — it cannot be as direct, as weird, or as assuming as content aimed purely at kids. The site loses the voice that makes the inoculation work.

**Why not an educator audience?**
If teachers are the primary audience, the site becomes a teaching tool — structured around lesson plans, learning outcomes, assessable skills. That framing makes the lesson visible. A visible lesson can be opted out of, argued with, resented. See C2-008 (Stealth Inoculation) — the lesson must not be announced.

**Why not design for adult learners who think like kids?**
That is a real audience but it is not the primary one. Adults can benefit from the content; the site does not exclude them. But optimizing for adult sensibilities would smooth the content in ways that undermine the anti-smoothing principle (see C2-007).

---

## Breaks If

Content begins addressing parents directly ("As a parent, you might want to..."). Any page explains the site's purpose in abstract developmental terms. The site acquires educational signaling: checkmarks, progress indicators, "you learned X today." The audience is reframed as "families" in any marketing or structural context.

---

## Tribal Context

**Operator supplied:** The formation-stage rationale — the insight that the inoculation works better at 8 than at 40.

**Session supplied:** The observation that direct address to kids enables structural weirdness that hedged family-audience content cannot sustain.

---

## Commit

**Decision:** Kids ages ~8–14 are the primary audience. The site speaks to them directly, not to their parents or teachers. Content register is set by this audience, not by adult approval-seeking.

**Confidence:** High.
