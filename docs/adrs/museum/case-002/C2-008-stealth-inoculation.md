# C2-008: Stealth Inoculation — The Lesson Is Never Named
**Method:** YY Method™ Home Edition v2.3 — Capture → Why → Why-Not → Commit → Timestamp
**Status:** Decided
**Date:** March 30, 2026
**Position in Hierarchy:** Derived — depends on C2-002 (Primary Audience), C2-007 (Anti-Smoothing)
**Freshness Boundary:** Active. Expires if the site's purpose must be disclosed for legal or regulatory reasons.

---

## Capture

The site never announces what it is teaching. No page explains the YY Method to kids. No entry begins "today's lesson is..." No sidebar describes "the cognitive skills you're building." The inoculation is structural — delivered through pattern repetition until the pattern becomes reflex.

The kid comes for YY. The kid stays for the next entry. The kid leaves having internalized, without instruction, that every conclusion has a rejected alternative, every claim has an expiry, and reasoning without provenance is a forgery.

---

## Why

Inoculation fails when visible. An announced lesson can be opted out of. It can be dismissed as adult content dressed in kid packaging. It can be argued with. It can be completed and forgotten. "I did the cognitive provenance lesson" is not the same as "I now automatically ask why-not."

The reflex is the goal. A reflex is not formed by being told about it. It is formed by encountering a pattern often enough that the absence of the pattern becomes noticeable. A kid who has read 40 entries structured around WHY-NOT will notice when a statement has no WHY-NOT. That noticing is the antibody. It cannot be delivered as a lesson.

The stealth is also the protection against the site becoming what it's defending against. A site that announces "we are inoculating you against cognitive theft" has already been smoothed — it's explained, positioned, branded. The inoculation works because the site just is the thing it's demonstrating, without explanation.

---

## Why-Not

**Why not include an "About the YY Method" page for kids?**
An explanation page makes the lesson visible. Kids who read "this site teaches you to think about why-not" will engage with the content differently — as students rather than as readers. The reflex forms through encounter, not through understanding the theory.

**Why not explain the site to parents on a separate page?**
A parent-facing explainer makes the content about parental approval. It also creates a framing that leaks — if parents know the site is "a cognitive training tool," that framing will appear in how they introduce it to kids. The site should be discoverable by kids without parental framing.

**Why not include subtle educational signaling (e.g., a YY "thinking tip" at the bottom of each entry)?**
Tips are lessons. A "thinking tip" at the bottom of every entry is an explicit curriculum delivered in small doses. It makes visible what should be structural. Even a single tip undermines the stealth.

**Why not add a "You've been reading YY's way" message after multiple visits?**
That would require tracking visits (violates C2-003) and would announce the mechanism precisely when the reflex is forming. Both violations are serious.

---

## Breaks If

Any page explains what the YY Method is to kids in abstract terms. Any entry includes explicit metacommentary about what the reader should be learning. The site acquires educational signaling: checkmarks, progress indicators, skill badges, completion states. Any external framing (press, promotional copy) describes the site as "teaching kids to think critically" in ways that flow back to kids who then approach the content as a lesson.
