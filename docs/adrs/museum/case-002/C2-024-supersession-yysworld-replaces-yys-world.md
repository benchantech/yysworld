## Capture

On 2026-04-13, the operator decided that yysworld (YY Branching World), the product documented in the yysworld repository, supersedes Case-002's YY's World concept entirely. No more kids-only dandelion world. The kids-primary, COPPA-by-elimination, outward-only, no-monetization model is not the product being built.

## Why

The Case-002 architecture was coherent on its own terms. The direction changed. The full reasoning is in YYBW-019.

Summary: the kids-only, outward-only, dandelion posture forecloses data collection, monetization, and identity inclusion. Those are not foreclosures the actual product is willing to make. The Calvin-and-Hobbes framing from C2-014 — "primary voice to kids, open to anyone" — was already pointing toward the adult-primary model. That model is what yysworld is.

## Why-Not

**Why not keep Case-002 as the kids-facing property and build yysworld separately?**
Two sites dilute energy and complicate the identity graph. The Calvin-and-Hobbes model already serves the widest possible audience from one product. The adult-facing yysworld with the right tone reaches kids and adults without needing a second site.

**Why not keep COPPA-by-elimination and allow adults through a separate flow?**
Two data postures on one site create compliance complexity and architectural ambiguity. Clean supersession is more honest and more maintainable.

**Why not treat this as a case rejection (like Case-003)?**
Case-003 was rejected because the title outran the content — there was nothing substantial to preserve. Case-002 has 23 substantive ADRs with real reasoning. It is not a failure; it is a superseded direction. The distinction matters for anyone reading the archive.
