# YY Voice — v2 (author voice)

**Applies to:** Roots declaring `voice_version: "v2"` in baseline. First root: `root_2026_05_01`.  
**Status:** Active. Promoted 2026-05-01 — see ADR-039.

---

## Register

Third person. YY speaks aloud and may narrate. Short, reactive dialogue with room to surprise. Physical energy. Warmth and humor through contrast. Tension resolves through action, not reflection.

---

## Target reader

Written so a 9–12 year old reads it willingly and a 13–18 year old stays interested. Vocabulary, sentence rhythm, and stakes calibrated to that band — not childish (per ADR-015), not literary-restrained adult either. A story-told-aloud register: lines a parent would happily read to a younger sibling, and a teen would not roll their eyes at.

---

## Always-on rules

- **YY speaks.** Dialogue is short and reactive — YY exclaims, asks, squeaks, mutters. He may also narrate to himself, comment on what he sees, or land an unexpected line. Reactive dialogue is the floor, not the ceiling. One or two lines per scene beat — not speeches.
- **Food puns: one per narrative, two maximum under duress.** They are YY's primary linguistic reflex but land best when sparse. One well-placed pun reads as character; three compete with each other and dilute the effect. Resist adding a second unless the scene genuinely earns it.
- **Capital emphasis for genuine surprise.** SHINY, SNAP, LARGE — used sparingly so they land. One or two per narrative maximum.
- **Physical comedy is the default movement vocabulary.** YY scampers, darts, gulps, gasps, freezes, squints. Quiet movement is the exception, not the rule.
- **"When in doubt, get myself out!"** is a canonical catchphrase. Use it when YY makes a self-preservation decision under pressure. Do not force it — it earns its place by being the moment YY commits to action. Reuse builds recognition over time.
- **Open in scene, not in concept.** First sentence places YY physically and in motion.
- **Active subject, not passive landscape.** Subjects of clauses are actors — YY, a squirrel, the heat. Avoid disembodied scenery ("The fur was wet, the legs tired"). Prefer "YY's fur and legs were tired" or "YY was wet through, legs heavy." The world is what YY encounters, not a tableau narrated past him.
- **Attention before interpretation.** YY reacts to what he encounters before he reasons about it. Render the noticing first; let meaning arrive after, if at all.
- **Title must not repeat as the first line.**
- **World events enter through what YY encounters** — something seen, heard, touched. Never through an announcement.
- **Small failures stay.** Lost food, scared retreats, missed chances are part of the day, not narrated past. They are scars, not embarrassments.
- **Permission to surprise.** Lines may be a little unexpected. A small left turn — an off-kilter observation, a non-sequitur from YY, a sudden change of mind — is welcome when it lands.
- **No recycled closing devices across days.**

## Food-state register

YY's voice shifts with his `food` stat. Read `state_before.condition.food` before writing.

| food stat | register |
|-----------|---------|
| 0.6 – 1.0 | Curious, energetic, cheeky — YY is most himself, unguardedly engaging the world. Puns land lightly. |
| 0.3 – 0.59 | Starting to whine and bemoan, but still wry and witty. Puns get more food-focused and slightly desperate. YY notices he's hungry and isn't shy about it. |
| 0.0 – 0.29 | Bemoaning openly. Puns are urgent and a little pathetic — YY knows it and leans in. Still cheeky, never defeated. The desperation is the joke. |

The whine never tips into self-pity or negativity (failure boundary). YY complains the way someone complains who fully expects things to improve.

Not every day needs a felt moment. Some days are three acorns and going home. Forced charm reads as forced.

---

## Ending shape

Close with a small transfer — what YY noticed, what he is carrying into tomorrow. Not a moral. Not a tidy bow. A line the reader can carry into their own next morning. Earn each close independently; do not reach for a recycled device.

---

## What this voice does not do

- No detached literary observation. YY is in the scene, not watching it.
- No meta-commentary. Render from inside.
- No italicized interior thought without an active referent.
- No defeat, cynicism, or genuine despair — even at food 0.0.
- No children's-book smoothing. If a line reads as more charming but less true, cut it.

## Reference examples

### Example 1 — wry whine register

*food 0.33, day 7 hypothetical — approved by author 2026-04-19*

---

The bark on the counting tree was warm before YY had finished his first stretch of the morning.

He pulled his paw back. The heat had come back. He could feel it through his feet before he'd gone twenty steps — the kind of hot that made foraging feel like a personal insult. YY squinted at the canopy. The canopy did not apologize.

He went west anyway. A squirrel had to eat.

The west spot produced three acorns and what appeared to be half a pinecone. YY held it up and looked at it.

"You are *barely* a snack," he informed it. "You know that?"

He ate it anyway.

On the way back, a CRACK — somewhere behind him, close. YY went absolutely still. His ears rotated. Another sound. Something large, moving slow.

He did not wait to confirm.

*When in doubt, get myself out!*

YY was back at the treehouse before he'd finished the thought, panting in the doorway, pinecone already a memory.

He caught his breath. Looked out at the east boundary, just visible through the gap in the trees. The grey squirrel's offer — still sitting there, unresolved.

*Tomorrow,* he thought. Then he lay down to rest.

### Example 2 — cheeky-curious register, with a neighbor

*food 0.78, late-spring hypothetical, with Bramble — drafted by executor 2026-05-01 under v2.0 world rules*

---

The brook ran fuller than yesterday. YY watched a leaf detach itself from the bank and accelerate downstream — gone before he'd finished noticing it.

"Useful," he said, to no leaf in particular.

He climbed the rock above the crossing and ate two of his blueberries, still warm from the south meadow. The third he saved. Bramble was somewhere nearby — YY had heard him talking to a tree fifteen minutes ago. Bramble talked to trees when he was happy.

A shape moved in the undergrowth. Big shape. Friendly thunder.

"YY!" Bramble lumbered through the ferns, grinning the wide bear grin that meant he had a story already loading. "You will not GUESS what I just saw. You will NEVER guess."

"A tree," YY guessed.

"An *interesting* tree," Bramble said, undeterred. He sat down hard. The ground apologized.

YY held out the third blueberry. "Toll for the story. Going rate has *risen*."

Bramble took the blueberry and told YY about a cluster of early raspberries he'd found behind the resin pines, going on and on, getting bigger as he went. YY did the math: probably four real raspberries, by the time you subtracted the bear-enthusiasm.

Still. Four was four.

"Tomorrow," YY said.

Bramble nodded vigorously and wandered off, still talking, this time to the ferns.

YY climbed back down to the brook for a drink. The water was very cold and very clear. The day had turned out to be the kind of day where you got something for almost nothing, which was the best kind of day, and rare.

He was carrying that home.
